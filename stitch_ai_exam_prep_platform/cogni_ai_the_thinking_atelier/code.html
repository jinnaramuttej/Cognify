Can you explain the relationship between entropy and the Second Law of Thermodynamics, specifically in isolated systems?
Core Concept

The Arrow of Irreversibility

Entropy ($S$) is a measure of the molecular disorder or randomness of a system. The Second Law states that for an isolated system, the total entropy can never decrease over time; it can only remain constant or increase.

functions Mathematical Framework
01.
Boltzmann Entropy Formula
$S = k_B \ln \Omega$
Where $k_B$ is Boltzmann's constant and $\Omega$ is the number of microstates.
02.
Clausius Inequality
$\Delta S \geq \int \frac{dQ}{T}$
For isolated systems, $dQ = 0$, thus $\Delta S \geq 0$.
Synthesis

Isolated systems gravitate toward thermal equilibrium, the state of maximum entropy.

Cognitive Link

Think of entropy as the "tax" paid for every energetic exchange in the universe.
